// The Problem

The idea is the easy part. The plan is where founders bleed.

You have a vision. You hire a developer. Six months later you've spent $150K and built the wrong thing for the wrong user with the wrong stack.

This isn't a talent problem. It's a sequencing problem. Most non-technical founders don't have access to senior technical strategy until it's too late — after the spend, after the hire, after the vibe-coded prototype that needs to be rebuilt from scratch.

VantageCTO gives you that strategic voice before the commitment.

How We Got to $276K

// Every line item sourced. Conservative estimates labeled.

Wrong-fit senior developer (6 months) $91K
6 mo. salary × 1.4× fully-loaded overhead
Average US senior developer salary: $130K/yr (BLS OES 15-1252). 6 months of base salary = $65K. Fully-loaded cost with benefits, payroll taxes, and equipment runs 1.4× base (8allocate) = $91K. Recruiting and onboarding costs for this hire are counted separately below.
Overbuild — unused features $40K
64% of avg. $62K build budget × wasted feature rate
The Standish Group found that 64% of all software features are rarely or never used. Applied to a modest $62K early-stage build, that's ~$40K in pure engineering waste — before accounting for ongoing maintenance of unused code.
Wrong architecture rewrite $91K
2 devs × 3 months × $130K/yr × 1.4× fully-loaded overhead
Architecture rewrites rarely fall to one person. Two developers spending 3 months rearchitecting a system (a conservative estimate for any meaningful rewrite) at BLS average senior dev salary of $130K/yr, fully loaded at 1.4× (8allocate) = $15,167/mo × 2 devs × 3 mo. = $91K. Does not include product delays or lost sprint velocity.
Delayed pivot — foregone MRR $30K
6 months × $5K/mo early MRR — opportunity cost estimate
The Startup Genome Project found that startups pivoting 1–2 times raise 2.5× more capital and show 3.6× better user growth than those that never pivot. Every month a necessary pivot is delayed has compounding downstream cost. $5K/mo is a deliberately conservative early MRR target — this figure is an opportunity cost estimate, not a cited statistic. Real-world cost is typically higher.
Recruiting & onboarding costs $24K
External recruiter fee (15% of $130K) + onboarding overhead
SHRM data puts average cost-per-hire at $4,700, but for senior technical roles using external recruiters (15–20% of salary), fees alone reach $19,500–$26,000. Combined with onboarding overhead ($4,100 average per DECODE), $24K reflects a single senior hire at the conservative end — and the wrong-fit scenario above implies at least two hire cycles.
Total Preventable Burn $276K

All figures represent conservative estimates for a typical early-stage US tech startup with 1–3 developers. Actual costs vary by team size, location, and sector. Where a figure is an estimate rather than a directly cited statistic, it is labeled as such above. Source links open in a new tab.
